Other Methods for Neutralizing

Carbon capture and storage (CCS)

  1. Carbon capture and storage (CCS) is a process in which carbon dioxide (CO2) is separated from other gases generated during mining activities.
  2. Subsequently, the captured CO2 is compressed and conveyed through pipelines, road transport, or ships to be injected into deep underground rock formations, such as depleted oil and gas reservoirs or saline aquifers.
  3. Furthermore, there exists a related concept known as CCUS,Carbon Capture Utilisation and Storage. In which, rather than simply storing CO2, it can be repurposed within industrial processes by transforming it into various products, such as plastics, concrete, or biofuels.

Advanced mining techniques

  1. Switching to electric mining machinery like shovels, drills, and excavators powered by renewable energy sources significantly reduces greenhouse gas emissions and improves air quality within the mine.
  2. integration of technologies such as high-precision excavation and automated systems presents opportunities to minimize disruption and energy usage.
  3. implementation of precise blasting techniques can efficiently fragment rock, thereby reducing the energy required for excavation and transportation.

Mine Reclamation

  1. Contouring of Land :Contouring is the stage in which the mined land is reshaped to resemble pre-mining topography and drainage.This is done by filling the excavation with rocky material.
  2. Layering of Soil: placement of topsoil ,that was stored before mining, or an approved substitute on the graded area
  3. Reseeding: The area is reseeded with native vegetation, crops, or trees.
  4. Monitoring: The area is carefully monitored for years.
  5. Revegetating and Reclaiming the disturbed land after mining helps in restoring ecosystems and potentially absorbing carbon dioxide through plant growth.
