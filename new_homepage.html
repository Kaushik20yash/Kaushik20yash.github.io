Carbon Footprints

A carbon footprint measures the greenhouse gases (GHGs) released by human activities like burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and consumption patterns. It's usually measured in tons of CO2 equivalent (tCO2e) and has two types: direct (e.g., burning fuel) and indirect (e.g., production and transportation of goods). Reducing our carbon footprint is crucial for fighting climate change and promoting sustainability.

Carbon emmision from coal mines

-India is the 3rd largest coal producer in the world, accounting for around 8% of global coal production.

- In 2020, India's coal mines emitted approximately 448 million tons of CO2 equivalent (tCO2e).

- The coal sector accounts for around 60% of India's total greenhouse gas emissions.

- The largest coal-producing states in India, such as Jharkhand, Odisha, and Chhattisgarh, contribute significantly to the country's carbon footprint.

- India aims to reduce its carbon intensity by 33-35% by 2030 compared to 2005 levels, as part of its Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) under the Paris Agreement.

Carbon Neutrality

A carbon neutrality pathway refers to the steps and strategies a country, organization, or individual takes to reduce their net carbon emissions to zero, balancing the amount of carbon dioxide (CO2) released into the atmosphere with an equivalent amount of CO2 removed or offset.


The pathway typically involves:

1. Reducing emissions through energy efficiency, renewable energy, and low-carbon technologies.

2. Transitioning to low-carbon or carbon-neutral energy sources.

3. Implementing carbon capture, utilization, and storage (CCUS) technologies.

4. Offsetting remaining emissions through carbon sinks, like reforestation or soil carbon sequestration.

5. Monitoring and reporting progress towards carbon neutralization.

SDG'S and climate goals

The SDG refers to the sustainable development goals and climate goals set up by the United Nations Organization in order to achieve a sustainable future by 2030. It address the environmental, economical and social challenges faced by different country. There exists a total of 17 SDG’s to be achieved by countries that are related to infrastructure, innovation,clean energy, sustainability , responsible consumption and more.
